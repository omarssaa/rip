Vectors are quantities that require a magnitude and a direction.

A vector can be divided into two components: the horizontal (x) component and the vertical (y) component.

\(V_x = V cosθ \)

\(V_y = V sinθ\)

In which:
\(V_x\) is the x component (the symbol change according to the quantity but the subscript remains).
\(V_y\) is the y component (the symbol change according to the quantity but the subscript remains).
\(V\) is the vector (the symbol change according to the quantity).
θ is the angle between the vector and the horizontal (x) axis.

If we were to use the angle between the vector and the y axis, we would switch the sin and cos in the equations, so:

\(V_x = V sinθ \)

\(V_y = V cosθ\)

\(V_x\) is the x component (the symbol change according to the quantity but the subscript remains).
\(V_y\) is the y component (the symbol change according to the quantity but the subscript remains).
\(V\) is the vector (the symbol change according to the quantity).
θ is the angle between the vector and the vertical (y) axis.
