The kinematics equations are a set of four equations that can be utilized to predict unknown information about an object's motion if other information is known.
In other words they are equations that link the velocity, acceleration, time and displacement.

The equations for linear kinematics are:
1) \(v = v_0 + at\)
2) \(Δx = (\frac{v + v_0}{2})t\)
3) \(Δx = v_0t + at^2\)
4) \(v^2 = v_0^2 + 2aΔx\)
In which:
⇒\(Δx\) is displacement (or position)
⇒\(v\) is final velocity
⇒\(v_0\) is initial velocity
⇒\(a\) is acceleration
⇒\(t\) is time
